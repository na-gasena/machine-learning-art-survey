# Survey Map

## Provisional Description

Machine-learning-related art, performance, and creative practice should be understood through process and relation, not only through visual style. In one work, machine learning generates images or sounds. In another, it transforms gesture or voice. In another, datasets, classification, copyright, and provenance become the object of critique.

## Categories

### 1. History, Symbolic AI, and Computer Art

AARON and The Painting Fool differ from contemporary deep generative models, but they prepared core questions of machine creativity, authorship, and autonomy. Computer art, algorithmic art, artificial life, and robotic painting connect to this lineage.

Representative items: Love Letters, ILLIAC Suite, Cybernetic Serendipity, AARON, Mutator, Interactive Plant Growing, Galápagos, The Painting Fool.

### 2. Neural Image Generation

This category includes DeepDream, neural style transfer, GANs, CAN, ArtGAN, latent diffusion, Stable Diffusion, and DALL-E. Image generation is both technical research and a creative environment made of prompts, models, datasets, user communities, and interfaces.

Representative items: DeepDream, neural style transfer, pix2pix, CycleGAN, BigGAN, StyleGAN, CLIP, VQGAN, DDPM, Stable Diffusion, DALL-E, Artbreeder.

### 3. Music, Performance, and Interactive Machine Learning

This category includes Wekinator, Magenta, NSynth, and research that treats machine learning as a musical interface. The key issue is not only the finished artifact but live response, gesture, iterative training, and improvisation.

Representative items: Continuator, Wekinator, Magenta, NSynth, DeepBach, Flow Machines, Shimon, Dadabots, FluCoMa, Living Archive, Improbotics.

### 4. Human-Machine Collaboration and Embodied Practice

This category treats AI as collaborator, instrument, voice, robot, or bodily extension rather than only as an autonomous output generator. Learning to See, Drawing Operations, Holly+, PROTO/Spawn, and Mosaic Virus belong here.

Representative items: Learning to See, Drawing Operations, Holly+, PROTO/Spawn, Living Archive, discrete figures, Alter3, Android Opera, In Love With the World.

### 5. Datasets, Art History, and Machine Vision

Datasets such as ImageNet, WikiArt, LAION-5B, ArtEmis, and NSynth are not neutral raw material. They are institutions of classification, collection, rights, and cultural bias. Computational art history and generative artworks meet here.

Representative items: ImageNet, WikiArt, LAION-5B, ArtEmis, OmniArt, SemArt, ArtGraph, ArtBench, BAM, The Met Open Access, Rijksmuseum Data Services.

### 6. Critique, Rights, and Provenance

This category includes ImageNet Roulette, Glaze, C2PA, the U.S. Copyright Office AI reports, and artist-opinion surveys. The creative possibilities of generative AI cannot be separated from consent, compensation, transparency, labor, provenance, and authenticity.

Representative items: ImageNet Roulette, Foregrounding Artist Opinions, Glaze, Nightshade, Have I Been Trained?, Data Provenance Initiative, C2PA, U.S. Copyright Office reports, EU AI Act training summaries.

### 7. Creative Tools, Education, and Platforms

This category includes Wekinator, ml5.js, Teachable Machine, ml4a, Magenta, and Runway. These systems turn research into tools, workshops, classrooms, commercial production, and film/advertising/game workflows.

Representative items: Wekinator, ml5.js, Teachable Machine, ml4a, Magenta, FluCoMa, Flow Machines, Artbreeder, Runway.

### 8. Regional, Cultural, and Critical Practices

This category covers practices easily missed by an Anglophone image-generation history. Stephanie Dinkins, Miao Ying, Naoko Tosa, Nao Tokui, Jake Elwes, Lauren Lee McCarthy, Sofia Crespo, Minne Atairu, Linda Dounia Rebeiz, Niceaunties, and Ouchhh Studio treat AI as a question of community, language, gender, family history, platform culture, public space, colonial archives, and performance.

Representative items: Not the Only One, Conversations with Bina48, The Zizi Project, Surplus Intelligence, Neural Zoo, Reimagining Benin Bronzes, Botannica Tirannica, Zoom Pavilion, AIDOL, Emergent Patterns, Making Kin with Machines, Afropean Intelligence.

### 9. Language, Literature, and Narrative Generation

Rule-based generators, neural language models, and LLMs become books, films, poetry, public sculpture, improvisation, and plays. The category follows corpora, editing, translation, actors, publication, and performance rather than generated sentences alone.

Representative items: Love Letters, Racter, word.camera, Sunspring, 1 the Road, Please Feed the Lions, Improbotics, The Day a Computer Writes a Novel, THEaiTRE.

### 10. Moving Image, Games, and Spatial Media

Game engines, artificial life, CGI film, robot groups, recognition, fMRI, projection, and sensors produce systems that change in time and space. Update rules, worldbuilding, intervention, surveillance, and re-presentation matter more than still-image quality.

Representative items: BOB, UUmwelt, AIDOL, This is the Future / Power Plants, In Love With the World, Zoom Pavilion, Archive Dreaming.

### 11. Exhibitions, Institutions, Markets, and Preservation

Exhibitions, conferences, prizes, auctions, online platforms, collections, and conservation make categories and values. Institutions are treated as agents of visibility, evaluation, ownership, and historical narration.

Representative items: Cybernetic Serendipity, SIGGRAPH Art Gallery, Prix Ars Electronica AI & Life Art, AI: More than Human, Neurones, Uncanny Valley, Run the Code, Christie's Edmond de Belamy auction, Guggenheim CCBA.

## Relations Between Categories

Historical AI art is a prehistory of current questions of machine creativity. Neural image generation depends on datasets and computation. Music and performance, language and narrative, and moving-image systems operate on different creative units: sound, text, and temporal worlds. Tools translate research into environments for artists and students. Global practices question universality and foreground language, community, and embodiment. Rights and provenance cross every category, while exhibitions, markets, and conservation determine what remains visible.

## Boundary Areas

Commercial production, advertising, and game asset generation sit between creative practice and industrial application. Immersive AI museum installations can blur artwork, research demo, and branded experience. Datasets can be research resources, artistic material, critical objects, and legal risk. Authorship in AI-assisted works depends on jurisdiction and the degree of human creative contribution. Educational tools can democratize access while also hiding model limitations and dataset bias.
