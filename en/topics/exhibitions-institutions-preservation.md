# Exhibitions, Institutions, Markets, and Preservation

<div class="topic-lead" markdown="1">
## Conclusion

The history of AI art is also a history of selection, display, documentation, sale, collection, and re-presentation. Cybernetic Serendipity in 1968, SIGGRAPH art shows since 1981, Ars Electronica, major AI exhibitions after 2019, auctions after 2018, and museum conservation programs each produce different boundaries for “AI art.” Institutions are active agents of valuation, visibility, resources, and historical narration.
</div>

## Three Points

- **Exhibitions make categories.** Placement under AI, artificial life, data art, or computer art shapes later histories.
- **A market “first” is not a technical first.** Auction claims must be checked against prior works, code, data, and labor.
- **Preservation is more than file storage.** Hardware, dependencies, weights, data, networks, interaction, and artist intent must be documented.

## Exhibition History

### Cybernetic Serendipity

Jasia Reichardt's 1968 ICA exhibition placed computers, music, poetry, visual art, and cybernetics in one international exhibition. More than 130 participants and roughly 60,000 visitors were recorded. Its own framing emphasized possibilities rather than achievements. [ICA archive](https://archive.ica.art/whats-on/cybernetic-serendipity-documentation/)

### SIGGRAPH Art Gallery and Archive

The program developed from the 1981 Frame Buffer Show and provides a continuing record across algorithmic, 3D, interactive, networked, and AI art. [ACM SIGGRAPH Art Show Archives](https://digitalartarchive.siggraph.org/)

### Major AI Exhibitions, 2019-2020

[AI: More than Human](https://www.barbican.org.uk/whats-on/2019/event/ai-more-than-human) connected historical narratives, science, and interactive art. Ars Electronica introduced its AI & Life Art category in 2019 and received 840 submissions in its first year. [Neurones, les intelligences simulées](https://www.centrepompidou.fr/en/ressources/media/r4Ep77X) presented a fifty-year archaeology of AI. Uncanny Valley at the de Young emphasized recognition, data extraction, labor, and prediction.

### Run the Code

The Thoma Foundation and Blanton Museum placed AI alongside real-time data, software, and longer histories of algorithmic art. [Exhibition archive](https://blantonmuseum.org/exhibition/run-the-code/)

## Markets and Collections

Christie's 2018 presentation of Edmond de Belamy was a market milestone, not the first AI artwork. Its framing also made the GAN inventors, reused code, WikiArt data, and earlier artists less visible. [Christie's announcement](https://press.christies.com/release-christies-announces-auction-of-an-artwork-created-by-an-algorithm/)

Mario Klingemann's Memories of Passersby I presented generation as an autonomous device rather than a fixed print, complicating what is owned. [Sotheby's archive](https://www.sothebys.com/en/auctions/ecatalogue/2019/contemporary-art-day-auction-l19021/lot.109.html)

Feral File connects online exhibitions, editions, and collecting. MoMA's exhibition and acquisition of Refik Anadol's Unsupervised foregrounded relationships among live generation, museum data, tokens, and display hardware. [Feral File history](https://feralfile.com/learn/about) · [MoMA](https://www.moma.org/magazine/articles/658)

## Preservation

The Guggenheim's [Conserving Computer-Based Art Initiative](https://www.guggenheim.org/conservation/the-conserving-computer-based-art-initiative) addresses hardware failure, obsolescence, and missing best practices. AI works add model weights, training data, inference environments, external APIs, and changing states to acquisition and conservation records.

Preservation records should include source and executable code; model weights and dependencies; data provenance and licenses; random seeds and mutable states; sensors, robots, displays, sound, and network services; artist-approved change; and visitor-data retention.

## Research and Practice Communities

- [ICCC](https://computationalcreativity.net/) covers computational creativity theory, evaluation, and systems.
- [AIMC](https://aimusiccreativity.org/) connects AI music researchers and practitioners.
- [NIME](https://www.nime.org/) provides a venue for new musical interfaces and interactive ML.
- [NeurIPS ML for Creativity and Design](https://neurips.cc/virtual/2021/workshop/21876) supported exchange around generative models and controllability.
- [SIGGRAPH](https://digitalartarchive.siggraph.org/) and [Ars Electronica](https://ars.electronica.art/archive/en/) provide major artwork archives.

## Comparative Questions

- Which works are made visible or excluded by exhibition and award categories?
- Are technical demos, research outputs, and artworks judged by one standard?
- How is scarcity constructed for reproducible and updating software?
- Do acquisition agreements cover models, data, APIs, retraining, and visitor data?
- Are inaccessible works being overvalued through promotional images alone?
