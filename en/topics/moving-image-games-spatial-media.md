# Moving Image, Games, and Spatial Media

<div class="topic-lead" markdown="1">
## Conclusion

Machine-learning art cannot be reduced to generated still images. When moving image, game engines, artificial life, robotics, projection, and sensors are combined, the artwork becomes a system that behaves over time, tracks visitors, and responds to an environment. Update rules, worldbuilding, spatial design, and the visitor's position matter as much as output quality.
</div>

## Three Points

- **Time is a medium.** Works appear as real-time generation, repetition, prediction, growth, and failure.
- **Game engines are exhibition systems.** They integrate agents, physics, cameras, and explorable worlds.
- **Immersion and surveillance are adjacent.** Face, gaze, and position data enable participation while importing classification and tracking.

## Map

## Key Cases

### BOB (Bag of Beliefs)

<p class="item-meta">2018-2019 · Artificial life/live simulation · Foundational · Confidence: High</p>

Ian Cheng built an artificial lifeform whose competing motivations, beliefs, and emotional life can be influenced by visitors. [BOB](https://bobs.ai/) · [Serpentine](https://www.serpentinegalleries.org/whats-on/ian-cheng-bob/)

### UUmwelt

<p class="item-meta">2018-present · Installation · Important · Confidence: High</p>

Pierre Huyghe worked with Kamitani Lab and ATR to exhibit machine-learning reconstructions derived from fMRI recordings. Gaze and biological inputs affect the environment. It is a model-mediated reconstruction, not literal mind reading. [MoMA](https://press.moma.org/film-media/uumwelt/)

### AIDOL

<p class="item-meta">2019 · CGI feature film/music · Important · Confidence: High</p>

Lawrence Lek's 83-minute science-fiction musical follows an aspiring AI songwriter and embeds originality, labor, corporate power, and Sinofuturism in a game-like world. [Work and trailer](https://www.lawrencelek.com/works/aidol)

### This is the Future / Power Plants

<p class="item-meta">2019 · Film/installation · Important · Confidence: High</p>

Hito Steyerl uses predicted future plants to question the desire to control the future through neural networks. [Portland Art Museum](https://portlandartmuseum.org/event/hito-steyerl-this-is-the-future/)

### In Love With the World

<p class="item-meta">2021 · Robotic installation · Important · Confidence: High</p>

Anicka Yi populated Tate Modern's Turbine Hall with responsive floating machines, combining soft robotics, AI, scent, air, and architecture into an imagined ecosystem. [Official announcement](https://www.hyundai.com/worldwide/en/newsroom/detail/0000000456)

### Zoom Pavilion

<p class="item-meta">2015 · Interactive installation · Important · Confidence: High</p>

Rafael Lozano-Hemmer and Krzysztof Wodiczko use face recognition and robotic cameras to magnify and track visitors, making spectators simultaneously viewers and detection targets. [Artist archive with media](https://www.lozano-hemmer.com/artworks/zoom_pavilion.php)

### Archive Dreaming

<p class="item-meta">2017 · Data sculpture/immersive installation · Important · Confidence: High</p>

Refik Anadol Studio transformed a Turkish cultural archive into a navigable space of machine-learned relationships. [Oxford exhibition archive](https://www.schwarzmancentre.ox.ac.uk/whats-on/refik-anadol-archive-dreaming-1s6r)

## Comparative Questions

- Is a state preserved or regenerated at each presentation?
- Is visitor data ephemeral, recorded, or used for learning?
- Does immersion offer agency, or only scale and spectacle?
- Is AI a production technique, character, institution, or operational layer?
- Who maintains the work when engines, APIs, and sensors become obsolete?
