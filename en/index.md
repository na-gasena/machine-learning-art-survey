# Machine-Learning-Related Art, Performance, and Creative Practice

## Overview

This survey organizes how machine learning has entered artmaking, performance, music, exhibition, critique, and institutional practice. The field is not simply a matter of "AI making pictures." It is a composite field involving creative agency, datasets, bodies, voices, tools, audiences, rights, provenance, and environmental cost.

## Scope

The survey includes papers, books, artworks, exhibitions, datasets, software, projects, standards, concepts, and historical cases. It prioritizes primary or reliable sources. Weakly verified information is marked as `Unknown` or with lower confidence.

## Field Map

The overall map is in [Survey Map](survey-map.md). The major categories are history and symbolic AI, neural image generation, music and performance, human-machine collaboration, datasets and machine vision, and critique, rights, and provenance.

## Timeline

- Late 1960s: Harold Cohen begins developing AARON.
- 2009: Early Wekinator; ImageNet paper.
- 2014: GAN paper.
- 2015: DeepDream and neural style transfer become widely visible.
- 2016: Magenta begins; ML-as-musical-interface arguments gain visibility.
- 2017: CAN, ArtGAN, NSynth.
- 2018-2019: Mosaic Virus, Edmond de Belamy, AI: More than Human, ImageNet Roulette.
- 2021-2022: DALL-E, LAION-5B, Stable Diffusion, Holly+, Unsupervised.
- 2023-2026: Glaze, broader C2PA adoption, Large Nature Model/Dataland, and heightened debates over rights and provenance.
